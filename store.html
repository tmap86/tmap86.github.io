Workstations

High-performance computer designed for intensive tasks such as video editing, 3D rendering, scientific simulations, and other applications that require significant processing power and memory. Workstations typically have more powerful processors, larger amounts of RAM and storage, and better graphics capabilities than standard desktop computers. They may also include specialized hardware for specific tasks, such as CAD (computer-aided design) or scientific instruments. Workstations are commonly used by professionals in fields such as engineering, architecture, graphic design, and animation.

Gaming PC

Optimized for playing video games with powerful graphics, fast processors, and plenty of memory and storage. It also comes with specialized components and features to enhance the gaming experience.

Custom PC

Custom PCs can be tailored to specific use cases, such as gaming, content creation, or scientific computing. They can also be designed for specific budgets, with users selecting components that balance performance and cost to meet their needs. The advantages of building a custom PC include greater flexibility and control over the system, the ability to upgrade components over time, and potentially lower costs compared to pre-built systems with similar specifications. However, building a custom PC requires some technical knowledge and may take more time and effort than buying a pre-built system.

Servers

Designed to provide critical services and resources to businesses. They are built with features such as redundant power supplies, hot-swappable components, and hardware-based management systems to ensure high levels of reliability, security, and scalability. They also have specialized software, such as virtualization software, to increase efficiency and reduce costs.
